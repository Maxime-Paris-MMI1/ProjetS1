GLOSSARY

Feminism Segregation
Bias Cold War
inequality Fundamental Sciences
Glass
ceiling Disparity
Feminism:
Feminism is a movement with values of women’s equality with men in all fields
Bias:
prejudices are all judgments that are formed in advance in relation to a thing or person
inequality:
Measurable differences in one thing (for example, standard of living) compared to people
Glass ceiling:
This means that the higher levels in hierarchical terms, are not accessible by a category of persons.
Segregation:
separating someone, a group of individuals, from a group of people
Cold War:
A period of war which began in the 1950s and which pitted the United States against the USSR. This war is special because it involves no real combat but just a confrontation with technological and ideological advances
Fundamental Sciences:
Fundamental sciences are all sciences that are based on the functioning and history of the world.
Disparity:
Lack of proportion. For example, in a field where there are more men than women, we will talk about disparity
